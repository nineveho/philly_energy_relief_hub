Applying an air seal can help achieve energy saving from 10-20% a year.
To Detect
Identifying air leakage involves two approaches:
  • Taking a visual inventory of potential problem areas, and noting actual air movement. Check to see if the caulking and weather stripping are applied properly, leaving no gaps or cracks, and are in good condition.
  • Building Pressurization test on cool, windy day (detect air leaks):
    • 1. Turn off all combustion appliances (such as gas burning furnaces and water heaters)
    • 2. Shut all windows, exterior doors, and fireplace flues.
    • 3. Turn on all exhaust fans that blow air outside (such as your clothes dryer, bathroom fans, or stove vents, or use a large window fan)
    • 4. Light an incense stick and carefully pass it around the edges of common leak sites. Or, use a damp hand.
To fix
Weatherstripping (preferred for indoor air quality) or caulking
Edges of Potential Leak Locations:
Interior:
  • 1. gaps along the baseboard
  • 2. edge of the flooring
  • 3. junctures of the walls and ceiling
  • 4. windows & doors
  • 5. lighting
  • 6. plumbing fixtures
  • 7. switches
  • 8. electrical outlet
  • 9. open fireplace dampers
  • 10. Wall- or window-mounted air conditioners.
  • 11. Attic hatches
  • 12. Where dryer vents pass through walls
  • 13. Vents and fans
Exterior
  • 1. All exterior corners
  • 2. Outdoor water faucets
  • 3. Where siding and chimneys meet
  • 4. Areas where the foundation and the bottom of exterior brick or siding meet.
